THE FIRST STEP

Disabled parking areas are the first step for handicapped individuals to venture away from home. It is difficult to step into the world and change their environment in daily life. Reaching the bus/train stations is really challenging for them. Therefore, handicapped individuals mostly wish to travel with their vehicles. In that case, they desperately need a specific parking area.

QUICK INFORMATION

  • You must have disabled parking badge to park in a disabled parking area.
  • You can park your vehicle up to three hours for free with specific badge.
  • If you have a disabled parking badge on your vehicle, anyone can use disabled parking spot to park your car but you have to be with them in vehicle.
  • If you park illegally in a disabled parking space you may face a fine.

WHY A SPECIFIC PARKING SPOT?

The reason is that they need a more space to getting in and off and move around their vehicles easily. For instance: it is not possible for them to get out of the vehicle with a walking stick and take wheelchairs or walk frame/walk assistant out of the trunk when they are in a ordinary parking spot.
